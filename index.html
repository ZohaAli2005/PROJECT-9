Museum History!

Eploring History & Culture

Culture, Features, Heritage, History, Travel

Pakistan, a nation graced with a rich tapestry of history and cultural diversity, proudly showcases its heritage in a collection of renowned museums.

8 Feb 2022

The Lahore Museum

The Lahore Museum, also known as the Central Museum, is one of the oldest and most significant museums in Pakistan. Established in 1865 during the British colonial period, it is located on The Mall Road in Lahore, Punjab. The museum's architecture is a beautiful blend of Mughal and British colonial styles, making it a landmark of historical and cultural importance The museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts spanning various periods, including the Indus Valley Civilisation, Gandhara art, and the Mughal era.

Established in 1865

Lok Virsa Museum

Dedicated to preserving and promoting Pakistan's diverse cultural heritage, the Lok Virsa Museum in Islamabad is a vibrant celebration of the country's folk traditions. From traditional crafts and musical instruments to vibrant costumes, the museum offers a colourful panorama of Pakistan's rich cultural mosaic. The living museum often hosts cultural events,The museum consists of several buildings as well as an outdoor museum which can accommodate up to 3000 visitors showcasing the living cultures of Pakistan.

Established in 1974

Pakistan Museum of Natural History

Nestled in the heart of the capital city, the Pakistan Museum of Natural History is a haven for both nature enthusiasts and history buffs. Its extensive collection spans prehistoric fossils to a diverse array of flora and fauna, offering a comprehensive overview of the country's natural wonders. The Dinosaur Gallery, a standout feature, showcases fossils dating back millions of years, providing a captivating glimpse into Pakistan's ancient past.

Established in 1976

What is Manzar features?

Manzar features over 200 artworks, showcasing modern and contemporary arts and architecture from Pakistan over the past eight decades. Designed by renowned architect Raza Ali Dada, the exhibition is titled Manzar.

Established in 1996
